Build, Launch + Grow, Together

Where open source meets startups

Don't let that repo sit around collecting dust. Together with the community at Assembly, you can polish it into a product, ship it, and grow a decentralized, community-owned business around it. Anyone will be able to jump in and help out, and all contributions you approve will be rewarded an ownership stake that translates to a portion of future profits.

Collaborate with the community

Maybe you've built something awesome, but you don't know how to talk about it. Maybe the back-end needs work. Maybe it'd be perfect for iOS, but you're a web person. Find and collaborate with people who have the skills to turn your idle side project into a glowing success.

You start out as the only person on the core team, so you guide vision, quality, and execution. Where this product goes is up to you, but a vast community of talented people are eager to work with you to get there.

Get paid

Building products collaboratively is fun and rewarding. But, in addition, when your product starts earning money, profits will be distributed to everyone who owns part of it. It's up to you how to build this business, but when you do, there's no ceiling to the earnings you can achieve.

If you have a side project that needs a bit more momentum, now's the time to get it going.

A few examples of side projects that became Assembly products:

  • Runbook

    Ben Cordell had built this product as a side project. It was functioning and had some active users but wasn't getting much traction. He brought it to Assembly, worked with the community to re-package the product, ramp up some features, design a new homepage and rewrite all the copy. Now, the product is taking off.

  • Really Good Emails

    Matthew Smith (@whale) loves really good emails. So, he put together a home for some really good emails that he comes across. That side project is now a business that brings in tens of thousands of dollars a year, and is built and owned by the community.

  • Octobox

    Octobox was a side project aimed to solve one problem for one person. Then, it became obvious that there were plenty of other people who would love for that problem to be solved. Now, with additional development, design, and marketing, Octobox is doing great.

How Assembly works:

Assembly products live beyond the confines of traditional companies. There are no investors, shareholders, or even employees. Instead, a global community creates and owns products, together.

The entire process is a collaborative effort, including vision, development, design, and marketing. Anyone can make a contribution and all contributions are rewarded with an ownership stake assigned within each team.

Collaborators participate in the success of their products. Each product has App Coins, which are earned by contributing work. When that product earns money, everyone gets a share of the profits.

Common questions

  • What kind of side project is good for this?

    Anything that might solve a problem for someone in the world other than you. Even if you don’t see it scaling to a massive customerbase, you might be able to help some people out and make some money from your hard work.

  • What if my side project is only half-built?

    That’s ok! Lots of products have gone from idea to launch on Assembly – it’s a perfect place to get development on your product moving.

  • What will happen if I put my side project on Assembly?

    You’ll have the chance to bring people along to work on it, and talented people from the Assembly community might be interested in working with you as well. If you or anyone else in the community figures out how to monetize your side project, the revenue will go to you and other contributors.

    You will be the leader of the product, and over time you can add more people to the core team if you'd like. You can build it how you like and at the pace you like, and it's up to you when and how to monetize it.

  • Who pays for hosting the product?

    Assembly will cover these basic costs before a product launches. At times members of the community will pay for them too. In any case, these expenditures will be tracked and reimbursed when the product earns profits.

    Once a product is live and earning revenue, monthly costs are paid before community earnings are distributed.

  • Am I giving up control of my product by putting it on Assembly?

    No. You will be the only member of the Core Team to start. This means you make all decisions regarding quality control, vision, and which contributions get merged into the product. Over time, you are welcome to add people to the Core Team if/when it makes sense to you. (We can also help you navigate these decisions, don't hesitate to reach out).

  • Who owns the intellectual property?

    You do. However, all products on Assembly are licensed under AGPL, and by agreeing to our terms of service you also are granting non-exclusive rights to Assembly to monetize this IP on behalf of the community (and you).

  • How much does Assembly cost?

    Assembly collects a 10% platform fee of the revenue after expenses. This is initially used to cover basic operating costs until a product’s revenue exceeds costs. Any proceeds after expenses are distributed to coin holders based on their app coins percentages. You can see Coderwall as an example of a mature product paying out in this model.

    We care deeply that we’re creating more value for the community than the 10% collected as a platform fee. Building on Assembly not only connects you with others around the world, but you also can build products with the freedom of not having to hand initial capital to run the product, managing the finances, paying accounting and legal costs and all the other burdens of operating a traditional company. We’re also investing in continuing to find ways to create new value for the community as well as exploring ways to reduce fees if feasible.
